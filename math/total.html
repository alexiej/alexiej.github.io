# Example 1

Probability that shoes are broken ( Event C) from factory A that has 75% market share ( P(A)=75% ) is 99% ( P(C|A) = 99%), from factory B is 95% that shoes are broken ( P(C|B)=95%). What is the probability that shoes are broken P(C)

Solution:

P(A)=75% P(B)=1−P(A)=25%

P(C)=P(C∣A)*P(A)+P(C∣B)*P(B)=99%*75%+95%*25%=98%
